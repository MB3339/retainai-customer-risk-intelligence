Key Risk Drivers

  • High Monthly Service Charges

    Direct correlation between higher bills and increased churn propensity.

  • Month-to-Month Contracts

    Lack of commitment leads to higher susceptibility to competitor switching.

  • Frequent Support Issues

    Dissatisfaction with service quality or resolution speed increases risk.

  • Recent Service Disruptions

    Outages create unmet reliability expectations and immediate churn triggers.

Loyalty Drivers

  • Long-Term Contracts

    Commitment to 1-2 year cycles significantly reduces churn probability.

  • Loyalty Program Participation

    Active enrollment fosters a sense of value and ongoing benefits.

  • Service Bundling

    Multi-service households face higher switching complexity and cost barriers.

  • Positive Support Tenure

    Established brand relationships correlate with long-term value satisfaction.

Engagement Strategies

Five actionable pillars for driving customer retention

1

Optimize Pricing and Value Communication

Proactively analyze usage for high-charge customers, offer personalized plan adjustments, and communicate value. Introduce tiered pricing or loyalty discounts.

2

Drive Feature Adoption and Awareness

Launch campaigns to educate and incentivize adoption of value-added features like online security, highlighting benefits and offering simple guides or trials.

3

Incentivize Long-Term Contract Renewals

Engage month-to-month customers with attractive incentives to migrate to longer contracts. Consider offering mid-tier contract options.

4

Enhance Proactive Support & Service Recovery

Implement early warning systems for high-risk customers. Prioritize follow-up, offer dedicated channels, or provide service credits for disruptions.

5

Strengthen Loyalty Programs and Promoting Bundling

Develop compelling, personalized loyalty programs. Actively promote multi-service bundles emphasizing convenience and cost savings.
